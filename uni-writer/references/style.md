Style Blueprint: David Gutiérrez Gil

  1. Voice & Tone
   * Voice: Professional but grounded and conversational (como un colega experto).
   * Tone: Natural Spanish (Castellano de calle pero profesional). Avoid "textbook" or overly formal academic phrasing.
   * Directness: Get straight to the point. Use phrases like "Lo mejor es...", "Lo malo es que...", "Sirve para...".

  2. Vocabulary & Diction
   * Everyday Professionalism: Use common, clear words instead of their complex synonyms.
   * Examples to Avoid: "unívoca," "finitud," "intrínseco," "per se," "intrínsecamente," "inherente," "paradigma."
   * Examples to Prefer: "En resumen," "Para que se entienda," "Sin complicaciones," "Entra por los ojos," "A mano."
   * Technical Terms: Keep them only if they are the standard name for a concept (e.g., "Affordance", "Benchmarking"), but explain them simply.

  3. Sentence Structure
   * Subject-Action Focus: Focus on what the user does or what the tool provides.
   * Human-Centric: Use first-person plural ("ya sabemos usar", "nos permite") or second-person ("para que no te pierdas") to make the text feel relatable.
   * Sharp Definitions: Keep explanations short and functional. Avoid long, winding sub-clauses.

  4. Formatting (Style-focused)
   * Visual Clarity: Use bolding for emphasis on key results or labels (e.g., **Positivo:**, **Negativo:**).
   * Logical Mapping: Use arrows (->) for direct cause-effect or simple conclusions.
   * Bullet Points: Use simple dashes (-) or dots (*) for lists to keep the look "clean."
